What is STEAM ?

It is a creative educational system based on the skill of solving problems through a combination of (Science, Technology, Engineering, Arts, and Mathematics) with the aim of reaching the student to possess skills such as investigation and analysis, dialogue and critical thinking. Science and technology are interpreted through engineering and arts in all Its forms are based on mathematical mathematical elements in a continuous learning process that does not stop with the end of the formal education stage so that learning becomes an enjoyable way of life.

How to design lessons according to the STEAM system ?

In order to provide lessons that stimulate student motivation, problem solving, creativity, critical thinking, entrepreneurship skills and teamwork, we must provide some indispensable steps,

from which we can clarify the steps of working on projects in Steam:

  • Start by preparing simple lessons
  • Prepare for a Steam project on the topic (unit of the week) that you will present to students during the week
  • The lesson should be related to the real world and not virtual.
  • Define clearly and concisely the challenge the students will tackle and help them define the challenge.
  • Use the engineering design process (EDP) to plan the project. This process is the approved system for solutions to various Steam challenges, starting to identify the problem and define it for innovation and development for the final solution to the problem.
  • The lesson is designed to support participatory group work and encourage working groups to develop their ideas.
  • Instruct the working groups to choose an idea to test and to prototype it.
  • Facilitate the process of pilot model testing and development.
  • Involve and encourage different work teams to share their results with each other.
  • Redesign if there is time.
